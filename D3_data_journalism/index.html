Correlations Discovered Between Healthcare Access & Income

There appears to be a correlation between the population that haus a lack of healthcare and the poverty rate for individual states. States that have a lower poverty rate also have higher rates of access to healthcare.

The data also seems to show two large grouping of states. The first grouping happens between a poverty rate of 11% and 14%, and the second groupoing happens between 15% and 17%. Both groups have a access to healthcare % between 6% and 8%.

There are two extremes in the datapoints. 12% of New Hamphires populations lacks healthcare, but they have a new zero poverty level. West Virginia and Kentucky have very high poverty levels, but a relatively low percenatge of the population has a lack of healthcare, compared to other states.
